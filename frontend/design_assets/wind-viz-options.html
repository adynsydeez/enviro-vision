Wind Visualisation Options

Same fire grid, four ways to show wind direction and magnitude. Drag the sliders to see how each responds.

N
Option 1 Recommended
Directional Ember Drift
Sparks fly downwind from burning cells. Speed controls travel distance. Most intuitive for kids — you literally see where fire is headed.
Highly readable Speed encoded in drift length Familiar (campfire sparks) Dense at high cell counts
Option 2
Elongated Glow Shadow
Each burning cell casts a stretched glow in the downwind direction. Elegant and subtle — the fire "leans" with the wind.
Low visual noise Works at any cell density Less obvious at low speeds Subtlety may confuse kids
Option 3
Wind Streak Lines
Short animated lines flow across the burning area in wind direction, like visible wind. Speed controls line velocity.
Wind feels tangible Speed clearly visible Busy over large fire areas Distracts from cell states
Option 4
Combined: Drift + Lean
Ember drift (Option 1) layered with the elongated glow (Option 2). Maximum clarity — two independent cues reinforce the same direction.
Strongest signal Redundant encoding Slightly heavier to render
